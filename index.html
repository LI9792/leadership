2. Overall Reflection on the Leadership Course (Before & After)

(1) Before Taking the Course: My Understanding of Leadership

Before taking the leadership course, my understanding of leadership was relatively straightforward. I believed leadership mainly came from “position” or “authority,” and it was more like a management skill: assigning tasks, arranging processes, and supervising results. I also used to think leadership was a trait that only a few people naturally had, while ordinary members had limited chances to lead.

(2) After Taking the Course: What I Learned and How I Changed

Through this course, I gradually realized that leadership is not only about “managing others,” but also about influencing a team, driving change, and supporting collective growth. A leader needs a clear sense of goals and values, as well as the ability to listen and communicate effectively, helping team members recognize their strengths and roles.

I also came to understand that leadership is not limited to formal roles in an organization. Everyone can show leadership in teamwork, such as coordinating tasks proactively, supporting peers, and providing direction and encouragement when challenges arise.

(3) Future Application Plan

  • Communicate more proactively in teamwork and clarify task allocation and timelines
  • Understand others’ perspectives first when conflicts occur, then seek solutions and consensus
  • Motivate the team through encouragement and recognition to strengthen engagement
  • Develop my organization, communication, and influence skills through learning and practice

4. Transformational Leadership Column

1. Definition

Transformational leadership is a leadership approach that influences team members through vision and shared values. It not only focuses on task completion, but also inspires internal motivation, encouraging individuals to go beyond personal interests and work together to promote organizational development and positive change.

2. Model and Key Dimensions (Four Core Dimensions)

(1) Idealized Influence

The leader serves as a role model, builds trust, and inspires followers to commit.

(2) Inspirational Motivation

The leader motivates the team through vision, goals, and encouraging communication.

(3) Intellectual Stimulation

The leader promotes creativity and critical thinking, encouraging new ideas and solutions.

(4) Individualized Consideration

The leader supports individual needs, provides guidance, and helps members grow.

3. Strengths and Weaknesses

✅ Strengths

  • Enhances motivation and strengthens team cohesion
  • Encourages innovation and supports organizational development
  • Focuses on personal growth and long-term talent development
  • Builds shared values and a sense of mission

⚠️ Weaknesses

  • Requires strong leadership competence and personal influence
  • Unrealistic visions may create implementation difficulties
  • Without institutional support, change may not be sustainable
  • Some team members may struggle with rapid or frequent change

4. Global Transformational Leaders (Examples)

Nelson Mandela

He promoted social transformation in South Africa through forgiveness, persistence, and a shared vision, demonstrating strong idealized influence and inspirational motivation.

Martin Luther King Jr.

He inspired millions through powerful vision-based speeches and played a key role in advancing the civil rights movement, showing strong transformational influence.

Barack Obama

He emphasized unity, hope, and change, motivating public engagement and reflecting inspirational motivation and rational communication.
